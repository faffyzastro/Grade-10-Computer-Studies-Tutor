# STRAND 1.0: INORGANIC CHEMISTRY

## Sub-strand 1.1: Introduction to Chemistry
**Content Summary:**
* Definition and branches of chemistry
* Careers in chemistry
* Chemistry in daily life (agriculture, medicine, industry, etc.)

**Specific Learning Outcomes:**
* Explain the meaning of Chemistry as a field of science
* Explore the role of Chemistry in day-to-day life
* Examine the effects of drug and substance use

**Suggested Learning Experiences:**
* Discuss meaning and branches of Chemistry with peers
* Brainstorm importance of Chemistry in daily lives
* Project: Develop posters to sensitize peers/community on risks of drug use

---

## Sub-strand 1.2: The Atom
**Content Summary:**
* Structure of the atom (Energy levels and orbitals)
* Isotopes
* s and p orbitals (first 20 elements)

**Specific Learning Outcomes:**
* Describe the structure of the atom
* Determine relative atomic mass of elements
* Write electron arrangement using s and p notation

**Suggested Learning Experiences:**
* Illustrate atom structure using Dalton, Rutherford, and Bohr models
* Draw electron arrangement for the first 20 elements
* Watch simulation on Rutherford Gold Foil experiment

---

## Sub-strand 1.3: The Periodic Table
**Content Summary:**
* Development of periodic table (Groups and periods)
* Ion formation (oxidation number, valency, radicals)
* Chemical equations and formulae

**Specific Learning Outcomes:**
* Relate element position to electron arrangement
* Derive formulae of compounds and write balanced equations

**Suggested Learning Experiences:**
* Arrange first 20 elements into groups and periods
* Predict ion types formed from given electron arrangements
* Practice writing formulae using valencies and oxidation states

---

## Sub-strand 1.4: Chemical Bonding
**Content Summary:**
* Octet rule and valence electrons
* Bond types: Ionic, covalent, dative, hydrogen, Van der Waals, metallic
* Lewis diagrams and resultant structures

**Specific Learning Outcomes:**
* Illustrate bond types in elements and molecules
* Investigate relationship between bond types and physical properties

**Suggested Learning Experiences:**
* Draw Lewis structures (dot and cross diagrams) for selected elements
* Explore uses of elements/compounds based on bond types (diamond, graphite, etc.)

## Sub-strand 1.5: Acids, Bases and Indicators
**Content Summary:**
* Definition of acids, bases, and indicators
* Types of indicators: Natural (plant extracts) and Commercial (litmus, phenolphthalein, methyl orange, universal indicator)
* The pH scale and its application
* Simple acid-base reactions and their applications in daily life

**Specific Learning Outcomes:**
* Explain the meaning of acids, bases, and indicators
* Prepare and use plant extracts as indicators
* Relate the pH scale to the acidity and alkalinity of a substance
* Discuss the application of acid-base reactions in daily life (e.g., medicine, agriculture)

**Suggested Learning Experiences:**
* Experiment: Use various plant parts (flowers, leaves, red cabbage) to prepare indicators
* Test common household substances (lemon juice, wood ash, soap, baking soda) using prepared and commercial indicators
* Use a pH meter or universal indicator to determine the pH of various soil samples

---

## Sub-strand 1.6: Salts
**Content Summary:**
* Definition and classification of salts (Normal and Acid salts)
* Solubility of common salts in water
* Methods of preparing soluble and insoluble salts
* Effect of heat on various salts (carbonates, nitrates, sulfates)

**Specific Learning Outcomes:**
* Define a salt and give examples
* Categorize salts based on solubility
* Describe and demonstrate the preparation of salts through various methods (Neutralization, Precipitation, Metal + Acid)
* Investigate the action of heat on various salts

**Suggested Learning Experiences:**
* Conduct experiments to prepare common salts like Copper (II) Sulfate or Sodium Chloride
* Carry out a precipitation reaction to prepare an insoluble salt like Barium Sulfate
* Observe and record color changes and gases evolved when heating salts like Lead (II) Nitrate or Copper (II) Carbonate

# STRAND 2.0: PHYSICAL CHEMISTRY (Continued)

## Sub-strand 2.1: The Gas Laws
**Content Summary:**
* Boyle’s Law, Charles’ Law, Combined Gas Law, and Graham’s Law of Diffusion

**Suggested Assessment Methods:**
* Observation of practical activities
* Written tests and quizzes on gas law calculations
* Project reports on real-life applications of gas laws

---

## Sub-strand 2.2: Mole Concept and Stoichiometry
**Content Summary:**
* The Mole, Molar Mass, Molar Volume, and Avogadro's Constant
* Empirical and Molecular Formulae
* Concentration of solutions (Molarity) and Stoichiometry

**Suggested Resources:**
* Digital simulations for molecular visualization
* Laboratory equipment: Balances, volumetric flasks, pipettes
* Charts showing the relationship between mass, moles, and volume

---

## Suggested Assessment Rubrics (Strand 2.0)

| Level | Performance Criterion |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Exceeding Expectations** | Accurately explains gas laws and mole concept; performs complex stoichiometric calculations with precision and relates them to industrial processes. |
| **Meeting Expectations** | Explains gas laws and mole concept; performs calculations and describes empirical/molecular formulae correctly. |
| **Approaching Expectations** | Explains some gas laws and basic mole concept; performs simple calculations with occasional errors. |
| **Below Expectations** | Identifies some aspects of gas laws and mole concept but requires significant support in calculations. |

## Non-Formal Activities & Community Service Learning

**Non-Formal Learning Activities:**
* **Chemistry Club:** Organize "Gas Law Challenges" (e.g., launching water rockets or predicting balloon expansion).
* **Science Fairs:** Demonstrating diffusion using colored water or scents in different temperatures.

**Community Service Learning (CSL):**
* **Consumer Awareness:** Testing the concentration of household cleaners or analyzing local water samples for salinity (linking Mole Concept to real-world purity).
* **Environmental Advocacy:** Sensitizing the community on the impact of gas emissions (linking Gas Laws to air quality).
