
# PRE-TECHNICAL STUDIES: FOUNDATIONS

## Essence Statement

Pre-Technical Studies is an integrated learning area comprising **Business, Computer, and Technical Studies**. It builds on Upper Primary Science and Technology to develop critical thinking, problem-solving, and financial literacy. It is anchored on **Kenya Vision 2030** and the **National ICT Policy (2020)** to prepare learners for industrial development and Senior School specialization.

### General Learning Outcomes

By the end of Junior School, the learner should be able to:

1. Communicate effectively through ICT.
2. Select and use tools/materials for production.
3. Use financial and entrepreneurial competencies for decision-making.
4. Observe safety in the immediate environment.
5. Create awareness of career pathways and self-development.

## STRAND 1.0: FOUNDATIONS OF PRE-TECHNICAL STUDIES

### 1.1 Safety on Raised Platforms

**Time Allocation:** 8 Lessons

**Sub-strand Overview:**
Learners identify types of raised platforms and understand the risks associated with working at height.

**Key Learning Outcomes:**

* Identify types of raised platforms (e.g., **ladders, trestles, steps, stands, mobile platforms, work benches, ramps**).
* Describe risks associated with working on these platforms.
* Demonstrate and appreciate safety precautions to minimize accidents.

**Key Inquiry Question:**

> What is the importance of observing safety when working on raised platforms?

---

### 1.2 Handling Hazardous Substances

**Time Allocation:** 9 Lessons

**Sub-strand Overview:**
Identification and safe management of dangerous materials found in the local environment.

**Classification of Substances:**

* **Poisonous:** Harmful if swallowed or absorbed.
* **Flammable:** Easily ignited.
* **Corrosive:** Can burn or destroy living tissue and materials.

**Safe Practices:**

* Reading and interpreting instruction labels and use conditions.
* Practicing safe handling of poisonous and corrosive substances.
* Discussing the importance of safety to prevent environmental and personal harm.

---

### 1.3 Self-Exploration and Career Development

**Time Allocation:** 6 Lessons

**Key Concepts:**

* **Nurturing Talents:** Identifying individual abilities through school clubs, societies, and planned activities.
* **Career Mapping:** Relating specific talents and abilities to professional pathways.
* **Ethics in Career:** Analyzing ethical vs. unethical practices in the professional world.
* **Decision Making:** Choosing a career path based on self-assessment for long-term development.

**Key Inquiry Question:**

> Why is self-exploration necessary for career development?

STRAND 2.0: COMMUNICATION IN PRE-TECHNICAL STUDIES
2.1 Oblique Projection
Time Allocation: 14 Lessons

Sub-strand Overview:
Learners are introduced to the characteristics and techniques of oblique drawing, a method used to represent three-dimensional objects in technical fields.

Key Learning Outcomes:
Explain the characteristics of oblique drawing in technical fields.

Sketch given drawings in oblique projection.

Draw shaped blocks in oblique projection using instruments (Cavalier and Cabinet).

Appreciate the application of oblique projection in technical drawing.

Core Concepts:
Types of Oblique Projection: Distinction between Cavalier (full depth) and Cabinet (half depth) drawings.

Geometrical Instruments: Proper use of set squares and rulers to draw shaped blocks.

2.2 Visual Programming
Time Allocation: 15 Lessons

Sub-strand Overview:
An introduction to creating interactive applications, games, and animations using visual programming software (e.g., block-based coding).

Key Learning Outcomes:
Explain the application areas of visual programming software in solving problems.

Create an application using visual programming for day-to-day life (games, stories, animations).

Embrace the use of visual programming in modern problem solving.

## **STRAND 4.0: TOOLS AND PRODUCTION**

### **4.1 Holding Tools**

**Time Allocation:** 12 Lessons

**Sub-strand Overview:**
Learners identify and practice the safe use of various tools designed to secure materials during production processes.

**Key Tools and Categories:**

* **Bench Vices:** Primary workshop tool for holding workpieces securely.
* **G-clamps and F-clamps:** Portable tools for clamping materials together or to a workbench.
* **Pliers:** Used for gripping, twisting, or cutting small components.

**Key Practices:**

* Proper maintenance of tools (cleaning, oiling, and ensuring smooth screw movement).
* Storage on shadow boards or tool racks to prevent damage and ensure easy access.

---

### **4.2 Driving Tools**

**Time Allocation:** 8 Lessons

**Sub-strand Overview:**
Focuses on tools used to apply force to other objects, such as driving nails, screws, or bolts.

**Key Concepts:**

* **Classification:** Striking tools (Hammers, Mallets) vs. Turning tools (Screwdrivers, Spanners).
* **Maintenance:** Ensuring handles are tight, cleaning striking faces, and replacing worn-out parts.
* **Safety:** Using the correct tool for the specific fastener to prevent slipping or injury.

---

### **4.3 Project**

**Time Allocation:** (Integrated)

**Sub-strand Overview:**
A practical application where learners integrate the use of holding and driving tools to create a functional item.

**Key Outcomes:**

* Applying safety protocols in a real-world production context.
* Demonstrating precision in marking out, holding, and assembling components.

---

### **SUGGESTED ASSESSMENT RUBRICS (TOOLS)**

**Indicator 1: Ability to identify and categorize holding and driving tools**

* **Exceeds Expectations:** Correctly identifies and categorizes more than ten tools with their specific functions and safety protocols.
* **Meets Expectations:** Correctly identifies and categorizes eight to ten tools.
* **Approaching Expectations:** Correctly identifies and categorizes five to seven tools.
* **Below Expectations:** Identifies less than five tools only with prompts.

**Indicator 2: Ability to demonstrate safety and maintenance of tools**

* **Exceeds Expectations:** Demonstrates advanced maintenance skills (e.g., handle replacement) and cites comprehensive safety protocols.
* **Meets Expectations:** Demonstrates basic maintenance and follows safety instructions.
* **Approaching Expectations:** Demonstrates maintenance skills only with significant supervision.
* **Below Expectations:** Requires constant prompts to handle tools safely.

---

### **Link to other Subjects:**

* **Mathematics:** Measurement and geometry applied in tool selection and project assembly.
* **Integrated Science:** Understanding the physics of force and leverage used in driving tools.
* **Agriculture:** Practical use of similar tools in farm maintenance and structure repairs.



STRAND 5.0: ENTREPRENEURSHIP
5.1 Financial Services
Time Allocation: 4 Lessons

Sub-strand Overview:
Identification and classification of financial institutions and the utilization of their services for entrepreneurial growth.

Key Concepts:

Classification of Institutions: Distinguishing between various providers including Banks, Insurance companies, SACCOs, and Micro-finance institutions.

Service Analysis: Evaluating the specific financial products offered by these institutions to support business operations.

Entrepreneurial Development: Learning how to effectively utilize financial services to scale and sustain a business venture.

5.2 Government and Business
Time Allocation: 6 Lessons

Sub-strand Overview:
Understanding the role of the government in the business environment, taxation systems, and digital service platforms.

Key Concepts:

Government Involvement: Reasons for state intervention in business to ensure fairness, equity, and social justice.

Taxation in Kenya: Exploring various tax types such as Income Tax, VAT, Corporate Tax, Fuel Levy, and Excise Duty.

e-Government Services: Utilizing digital platforms (like NTSA or e-Citizen) for business registration, licensing, and compliance.

5.3 Business Plan
Time Allocation: 6 Lessons

Sub-strand Overview:
The meaning, importance, and systematic preparation of a business plan for successful entrepreneurship.

Components of a Business Plan:

Executive Summary: A high-level overview of the business proposal.

Business Description: Details regarding the nature and goals of the venture.

Market/Competitor Analysis: Research on target customers and existing competition.

Product and Services: Description of what the business is selling.

Marketing Plan: Strategies for reaching and retaining customers.

Financial Projection: Forecasts of revenue, expenses, and profitability.
